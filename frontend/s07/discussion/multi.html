How Solar Panels Work

Turning sunlight into usable electricity in 3 simple steps

1. Photon Absorption

When sunlight hits the solar cell (usually made of silicon), it delivers packets of energy called photons. These photons are absorbed by the semiconducting material, transferring energy to the electrons within the cell.

2. The "Electron Breakout"

The energy from the photons knocks electrons loose from their atoms. Because solar cells have a built-in electric field, these electrons are pushed in a single direction. This movement creates Direct Current (DC) electricity.

3. Conversion and Distribution

The DC electricity flows into an inverter, which converts it into Alternating Current (AC). This usable electricity is then sent to your breaker box to power your home appliances.
