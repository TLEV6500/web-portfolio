ElectricVehicles

EVs convert electrical energy directly into motion with over 85% efficiency — more than four times better than conventional combustion engines, which top out around 20–30%.

How it works — Battery → Motor → Wheels

Step 01
🔋
Battery
A high-voltage lithium-ion pack stores electrical energy. An inverter converts this DC power into AC current, while the thermal management system keeps everything at safe operating temperatures.
Step 02
⚡
Motor
The electric traction motor receives AC power and produces instant torque from 0 RPM. During braking, it reverses role — acting as a generator to send energy back to the battery.
Step 03
🛞
Wheels
Torque is delivered through a single-speed reduction gear directly to the wheels. No multi-gear transmission needed — the wide RPM range of the motor handles all driving conditions.
